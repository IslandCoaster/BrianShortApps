# Proposal 003 — Financial Timeline & Lifecycle

**Status:** Draft  
**Sprint:** Sprint Two — First Private Application

---

## Purpose

The Financial Timeline records the user's financial life as a sequence of financial events over time.

The Financial Timeline is the permanent historical record.

The Financial Engine continuously interprets the timeline into the current Financial State.

---

## Core Principle

The Financial Timeline records what happened.

The Financial Engine determines what it means today.

---

## Financial Timeline

The Financial Timeline contains every meaningful event that changes the user's current or projected financial life.

Examples:

- Paycheck received
- Statement generated
- Bill received
- Payment completed
- Transaction imported
- Credit score updated
- Credit limit changed
- Interest charged
- Budget changed
- Goal changed

---

## Reports

Reports are historical interpretations of the Financial Timeline.

Monthly reports, yearly reports, credit history, budget history, and net worth history all derive from the same timeline.

Reports do not own independent truth.

---

## Trends

Trends are patterns detected across the Financial Timeline.

Examples:

- Paycheck variance
- Spending drift
- Utilization movement
- Credit score correlation
- Debt payoff acceleration
- Net worth growth

---

## Product Promise

The Personal Finance Operating Environment should not require the user to mentally reconstruct financial history.

The timeline preserves history.

The engine interprets it.

The operating environment explains it.

---

## Ownership

### Financial Timeline Owns

- Historical financial events
- Event order
- Lifecycle state
- Permanent financial history

### Financial Engine Owns

- Interpretation
- Recalculation
- Current Financial State
- Forecasts
- Recommendations
- Trends
