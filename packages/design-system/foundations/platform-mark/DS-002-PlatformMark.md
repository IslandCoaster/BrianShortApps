# DS-002 — BrianShortApps Platform Mark

## Status

Locked v1.0

## Purpose

The BrianShortApps Platform Mark is the visual architecture of the BrianShortApps ecosystem.

It is not the logo of a single application.

Every application lives within it, inherits from it, and contributes back to it.

## Core Philosophy

The platform never changes.

Only the center evolves.

The framework supports.

The application leads.

## Geometry

The Platform Mark uses three rings:

1. Outer Ring — Experience
2. Middle Ring — Products
3. Inner Ring — Progress

Each ring represents a permanent part of the BrianShortApps ecosystem.

## Outer Ring — Experience

The outer ring represents the shared user experience.

Every BrianShortApps product should evoke the same feeling:

- quality
- confidence
- clarity
- consistency
- professionalism

When a user opens a BrianShortApps application, they should already know what kind of experience to expect.

## Middle Ring — Products

The middle ring represents the applications that live inside the BrianShortApps experience.

Every product may solve a different problem, but no product should feel like it came from a different company.

Products are constrained by the experience, but not limited by it.

## Inner Ring — Progress

The inner ring represents forward motion.

BrianShortApps should continue to evolve through:

- better technology
- better design
- better infrastructure
- better products
- better user experiences

Progress remains grounded by experience and product identity.

## Access Breaks

The mark uses three access breaks.

The breaks represent entry into the ecosystem from multiple directions:

- different needs
- different professions
- different industries
- different products

One platform.

## Ring Break Rules

### Outer Ring

The outer ring has three breaks positioned at:

- 12 o'clock
- 4 o'clock
- 8 o'clock

### Middle Ring

The middle ring has three breaks offset from the outer ring.

The middle ring must not duplicate the outer ring breaks.

Its breaks sit at the midpoint positions between the outer ring breaks.

### Inner Ring

The inner ring has three breaks positioned at:

- 12 o'clock
- 4 o'clock
- 8 o'clock

One side of each inner-ring break may bevel inward to create subtle visual momentum toward the product zone.

## Product Zone

The center area is reserved for product identity.

The rings support the product.

The product owns the attention.

Platform-level uses may leave the product zone empty or use the BrianShortApps platform identity.

Application-level uses may place the product symbol inside the center zone.

## Product Accent Rule

The platform framework remains consistent.

Each application may express one product accent color.

Examples:

- BrianShortApps — Royal Blue
- MCO Satellite — Sky Blue
- Flight Log — Emerald
- Debt Planner — Violet

The symbol framework does not change per product.

Only the center identity and accent color evolve.

## Motion System

The Platform Mark is designed for motion.

Preferred motion sequence:

1. Outer ring resolves.
2. Middle ring resolves.
3. Inner ring resolves.
4. Product zone appears.
5. Mark locks into alignment.

Motion should communicate momentum, precision, and arrival.

Motion must remain restrained and professional.

## Usage Rules

The Platform Mark must work as:

- favicon
- application icon
- SVG
- print mark
- motion asset
- monochrome mark
- dark-mode mark
- light-mode mark

The mark should remain recognizable at small sizes.

## Restrictions

Do not:

- add letters into the mark
- use gradients in the core mark
- create application-specific ring geometry
- duplicate middle-ring gaps at 12, 4, and 8 o'clock
- overpower the product zone
- use MCO Satellite or other product branding for platform applications

## Implementation Ownership

The Platform Mark belongs to:

packages/design-system/assets/logos

Applications consume the mark from the Design System.

Applications do not own or duplicate the platform identity.

## Final Statement

The Platform Mark is not the logo of a single application.

It is the visual architecture of the BrianShortApps ecosystem.

Every application lives within it, inherits from it, and contributes back to it.
